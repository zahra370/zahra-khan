MS Research & Contributions

Thesis Title: Motion Planning of Nonholonomic Differential Drive Robot in Cluttered Environment using Deep Reinforcement Learning

Zahra's MS research focused on motion planning for nonholonomic differential drive robots in complex environments using Deep Reinforcement Learning (DRL). She designed a dense, SLAM-based reward function to improve navigation, obstacle avoidance, and recovery from local minima, major limitations in standard DRL methods.

She implemented and compared three advanced DRL algorithms: Soft Actor-Critic (SAC), Twin Delayed Deep Deterministic Policy Gradient (TD3), and Deep Deterministic Policy Gradient (DDPG). The algorithms were evaluated for convergence stability, smoothness, and goal-reaching accuracy.

The results demonstrated significant improvement in trajectory smoothness, path efficiency, and goal success rate, showing that integrating SLAM awareness into DRL enhances navigation in cluttered, real-world environments.
